Cajun Chicken Pasta (Pressure Cooker)


This was my favorite dish served at a cafeteria in college. The sauce is so creamy and the the flavor layers are both powerful and subtle. After colleges sent students home in March, the dining service Facebook account shared this recipe, and I adapted it for the pressure cooker by poking through some recipes on Pinterest. Cajun chicken pasta is so special to me and it was fun to eat it again after a few years and also share it with my parents. //luv, YA


Ingredients


• 2 chicken breasts, cooked and sliced
• 1.75 cup heavy cream
• 2 tsp neutral oil (canola, vegetable, etc)
• 1 red bell pepper, sliced
• 1 zucchini, chopped
• 1 box penne or rotini pasta
• 2 cloves garlic, minced
• 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
• 1/2 tsp cayenne
• 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
• 1/2 tsp salt
• 1/4 tsp black pepper
• 1 tsp Italian seasoning
• cilantro for garnish

Instructions


1. Chop the chicken breasts into strips. Season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.
2. Set pressure cooker to saute mode. Add chicken, garlic, pepper, and zucchini. Cook 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
3. Add broth and pasta. Stir and make sure liquid covers all pasta. If it does not, add water.
4. Make sure pressure valve is in sealed position, then set pressure cooker to manual/high for 5 minutes.
5. Once the timer is up, release pressure for 5 minutes.
6. Open the lid, then mix pasta. Add heavy cream and spices. Stir, then put on lid for 5 minutes.
7. After 5 minutes, stir again and serve with cilantro for garnish.
