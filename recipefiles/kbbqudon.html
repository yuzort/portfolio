Korean BBQ Steak and Udon Noodles


I've been really hungry for Korean barbecue steak because I went to a Korean barbecue place about a year ago. I loved the way they did the meats. I wanted to try to do that for myself. The meals that go outside of my comfort zone are the ones that I enjoy the most cooking. I'm using ingredients I've never used before so I don't know what the flavor palette will be like. I was very surprised at how good the ginger-garlic combination was. I've never used ginger and I've had it only in teas, so I didn't know what it adds to a meat or noodle. It was cool to explore this sweet-savory taste to the meat and noodles. //xoxo, AB


Ingredients


• 2 eggs
• green onions
• 1/2 red pepper, sliced
• 1/2 green pepper, sliced
• 1 clove garlic
• 1 clove ginger
• 12-15 baby carrots or 2 carrots
• 2 packages udon noodles
• steak
• 1.5-2.5 tbpsp Korean spice rub
• 2 tbsp Korean barbecue sauce
• 1 tbsp soy sauce
• 1 tbsp teriyaki sauce

Instructions


1. Combine spice rub, barbecue sauce, soy sauce, and teriyaki sauce for the rub.
2. Marinate the steak in the rub.
3. Sauté steak. Set aside.
4. Boil noodles.
5. Stir fry egg, green onions, red peppers, green peppers, garlic, ginger, and carrots.
6. Mix stir fry and udon noodles.
7. Serve with steak over the udon noodles.
8. Sprinkle chopped green onions on top for garnish.
